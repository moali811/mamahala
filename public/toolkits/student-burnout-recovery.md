## Section 1: Recognizing Burnout

### The Three Dimensions of Burnout

1. **Exhaustion:** Physical, emotional, and mental depletion. You feel tired even after resting.
2. **Cynicism:** Detachment and negativity toward your studies, activities, or relationships. Things that used to excite you now feel meaningless.
3. **Reduced efficacy:** The feeling that nothing you do makes a difference. Your confidence in your own abilities drops.

### Burnout Self-Assessment

Rate each statement from 1 (never) to 5 (always):

- I feel physically exhausted most days ___
- I dread going to class or doing schoolwork ___
- I have trouble concentrating even on things I enjoy ___
- I feel emotionally numb or disconnected ___
- I have withdrawn from friends or activities ___
- I get sick more often than usual ___
- I feel like my efforts do not matter ___
- I have trouble sleeping or sleep too much ___
- I feel irritable or short-tempered ___
- I cannot remember the last time I felt truly rested ___

**Scoring:** 10-20: Mild stress. 21-35: Warning signs. 36-50: Burnout territory, please seek support.

## Section 2: Understanding Your Burnout

### Common Causes for Students

- Academic overload without adequate breaks
- Perfectionism and fear of failure
- Lack of social support or meaningful connection
- Financial stress or working while studying
- Family expectations and cultural pressure
- Comparison with high-achieving peers
- Loss of autonomy, feeling like your life is not your own

### The Burnout Equation

Burnout = High Demands + Low Resources + Low Recovery

To recover, you need to address all three parts: reduce demands where possible, increase your resources and support, and prioritize recovery.

## Section 3: The Recovery Plan

### Week 1-2: Emergency Measures

These are immediate actions to stop the bleeding:

1. **Cancel one thing.** Look at your schedule and remove the lowest-priority commitment. Give yourself permission.
2. **Set a hard stop time each night.** No studying after this hour. Non-negotiable.
3. **Sleep.** This is not optional. Aim for 7-8 hours. Burnout recovery starts with rest.
4. **Move your body gently.** A 15-minute walk. Stretching. Yoga. Nothing intense.
5. **Reach out to one person.** Tell someone you trust how you are feeling.

### Week 3-4: Rebuilding

1. **Audit your commitments.** What can you drop, delegate, or postpone?
2. **Reintroduce one enjoyable activity.** Something that is not productive, just pleasurable.
3. **Create a sustainable study schedule.** One that includes breaks, buffer time, and days off.
4. **Practice saying no.** Start small. Build the muscle.
5. **Eat regular meals.** Burnout recovery requires fuel.

### Month 2+: Sustainable Habits

1. **Build recovery into your routine,** not as an afterthought but as a priority
2. **Set boundaries around your time and energy**
3. **Regularly check in with yourself:** "How am I doing, really?"
4. **Maintain your support network**
5. **Watch for relapse signs** and intervene early

## Section 4: Preventing Future Burnout

### The Sustainable Success Model

Success is not about grinding until you break. It is about finding a pace you can maintain over the long term. Ask yourself:

- "Can I maintain this pace for the next six months?"
- "Am I sacrificing my health for my grades?"
- "What would I tell a friend who was this exhausted?"

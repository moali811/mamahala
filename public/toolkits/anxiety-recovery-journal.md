# The Anxiety Recovery Journal

## A 90-Day Guided Journal Combining CBT, Gratitude & Cognitive Reframing

**A Toolkit by Dr. Hala Ali | Mama Hala Consulting**

---

> *This journal won't cure your anxiety. But it will teach you to stop being afraid of it.*

Anxiety tells you a lot of lies. It tells you that the worst will happen. It tells you that you can't handle it. It tells you that worrying keeps you safe, and that if you stop worrying, something terrible will slip through.

This journal is designed to help you catch those lies — gently, without judgment — and replace them with something closer to the truth. Over the next 90 days, you'll learn to observe your anxious thoughts without believing every one of them, to understand the patterns that keep your anxiety alive, and to build new habits that shrink the space anxiety occupies in your life.

This is not about becoming fearless. It's about becoming brave enough to live fully, even when anxiety is along for the ride.

---

## How to Use This Journal

- **5 minutes in the morning.** Set your intention. Name what you're grateful for. Ground yourself before the day begins.
- **5 minutes in the evening.** Reflect on what happened. Record your thoughts. Notice what was real and what was anxiety talking.
- **Be honest, not perfect.** You don't need to write beautifully. You don't need to fill every line. You just need to show up.
- **Don't skip days — but forgive yourself when you do.** If you miss a day, pick up where you left off. The streak doesn't matter. The practice does.
- **Keep this private.** This journal is for your eyes only. That freedom is what makes it work.

# Section 2: The CBT Toolkit

## Days 8-30

*Now that you understand your anxiety, it's time to start working with it. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is one of the most researched and effective approaches to anxiety. The core idea is simple: your thoughts shape your feelings, and your feelings shape your behavior. Change the thought, and the rest begins to shift.*

*For the next 23 days, you'll follow a daily structure of morning intention-setting and evening thought recording.*

Anxiety may still visit you. It probably will. But you are no longer the same person who opened this journal on Day 1. You have tools now. You have evidence that your worst fears usually don't come true. You have proof that you can feel afraid and do it anyway.

That's not the end of anxiety. It's the beginning of freedom.

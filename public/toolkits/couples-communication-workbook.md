# Couples Communication Workbook

## 30 Structured Exercises to Rebuild, Repair & Deepen Your Connection

**A Toolkit by Dr. Hala Ali | Mama Hala Consulting**

---

> *The quality of your relationship is the quality of your communication.*

Every couple argues. Every couple misunderstands each other. Every couple has moments where they look at the person across from them and think, "How did we get here?"

The difference between couples who thrive and couples who slowly drift apart isn't the absence of conflict — it's the presence of communication. Not perfect communication. Not therapy-speak. Just the willingness to keep turning toward each other, even when it's hard.

This workbook contains 30 structured exercises designed to help you and your partner rebuild dialogue, navigate conflict more skillfully, and deepen the emotional intimacy that brought you together in the first place. You don't have to do them in order. You don't have to do them all. Start with what feels accessible, and go from there.

---

## How to Use This Workbook

Before you begin, agree on these ground rules together:

1. **No interrupting.** When your partner is speaking, your job is to listen — not to prepare your response.
2. **No devices.** Put your phones in another room. This time is sacred.
3. **Take turns.** Each exercise has a structure. Follow it. Resist the urge to jump ahead.
4. **It's okay to pause.** If an exercise brings up strong emotions, stop. Breathe. Come back to it when you're ready. Pausing is not quitting.
5. **No scoring.** This isn't about who's right. It's about understanding.
6. **Confidentiality.** What's shared in these exercises stays between the two of you.
7. **Be kind.** You're on the same team, even when it doesn't feel that way.

**Time commitment:** Most exercises take 15-30 minutes. Try to do 2-3 per week.

### Exercise 12: The Repair Checklist

**Purpose:** Have ready-made phrases to de-escalate when conversations go sideways.

### Exercise 14: Identifying Your Conflict Style

**Purpose:** Understand how each of you responds to conflict so you can adjust.

**How it works:**
Read the four common conflict styles below. Each partner identifies their primary and secondary style.

1. **The Pursuer:** Wants to talk about it right now. Follows their partner to keep the conversation going. Feels anxious when issues are unresolved.

2. **The Withdrawer:** Needs space when things get heated. Shuts down or leaves the room. Feels overwhelmed by intense emotion.

3. **The Fixer:** Jumps straight to solutions. Wants to problem-solve immediately. Gets frustrated when their partner "just wants to vent."

4. **The Peacekeeper:** Avoids conflict at all costs. Agrees to things they don't mean. Builds resentment silently.

### Exercise 21: Questions That Build Closeness

**Purpose:** Rediscover each other through meaningful conversation.

**How it works:**
Take turns asking and answering. Go slowly. These aren't rapid-fire trivia — they're invitations.

**Set 1 — Getting Reacquainted:**
1. What's something you've changed your mind about in the last few years?
2. What's a memory from your childhood that shaped who you are?
3. If you could live anywhere for a year, where would you go?
4. What do you think I don't know about you yet?

**Set 2 — Going Deeper:**
5. What are you most afraid of losing?
6. When do you feel most alone, even when I'm right here?
7. What's the bravest thing you've ever done?
8. What do you wish you could tell your younger self?

**Set 3 — Us:**
9. What's the best thing I've ever done for you?
10. What do you think our relationship has taught you?
11. If our relationship were a book, what chapter are we in?

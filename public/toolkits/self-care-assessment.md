# Self-Care Assessment & Planning Guide

**A Toolkit by Dr. Hala Ali | Mama Hala Consulting**

---

> *Self-care isn't selfish. It's how you stay available for the people who need you.*

When you're running on empty, everyone around you feels it — your children, your partner, your colleagues. You can't pour from an empty cup, and you certainly can't show up as the parent, partner, or person you want to be when you're depleted.

This guide will help you honestly assess where you stand across six key dimensions of well-being, identify the areas that need the most attention, and build a realistic plan that fits your actual life — not someone else's idea of what self-care should look like.

Self-care doesn't have to mean spa days and weekend retreats. It can be five minutes of quiet before the house wakes up. It can be saying no to one thing this week. It can be going to bed thirty minutes earlier. What matters is that it's intentional, consistent, and yours.

---

## Section 1: The 6-Dimension Self-Care Assessment

For each dimension below, read the reflection questions carefully, then rate yourself on a scale of **1 to 5**:

- **1** = I'm really struggling here
- **2** = I'm not doing well in this area
- **3** = I'm managing, but just barely
- **4** = I'm doing fairly well
- **5** = I feel strong and consistent here

Be honest. No one is reading this but you.

---

### What Your Score Means

**24-30: Thriving**
You're doing well. You've built habits and awareness that support your well-being across most areas of life. Keep it up — and pay attention to any dimension that scored below a 4. Even small dips, left unattended, can grow into bigger problems over time.

**18-23: Steady but Stretched**
You're managing, but there are cracks forming. Some areas of your life are getting what they need; others are being neglected. This is the most common range — and the most important time to act. You're not in crisis, but you're heading toward burnout if you don't make intentional changes.

**12-17: Needs Serious Attention**
You're running on fumes. Multiple areas of your life are underfed, and you're probably feeling the effects — exhaustion, irritability, disconnection, or a persistent sense that something is wrong. This score is a signal, not a sentence. It means it's time to ask for help and start making even small changes.

**6-11: Burnout Risk**
You are depleted. This isn't about willpower or trying harder — it's about getting support. Please consider talking to a professional. You deserve care, and you don't have to figure this out alone. This score often comes with shame, but there's no shame in being honest about where you are. That honesty is the first step toward recovery.

## A Note Before You Go

Self-care isn't a one-time exercise. It's a practice — something you return to again and again as your life changes. The person you were when you started this guide isn't the same person who will pick it up next month or next year. Your needs will shift. Your capacity will change. What stays the same is the commitment to showing up for yourself so you can show up for the people you love.

If this assessment revealed that you're struggling more than you realized, please know: that awareness is not failure. It's courage. And you don't have to carry it alone.
